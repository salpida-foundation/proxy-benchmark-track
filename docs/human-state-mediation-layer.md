# Human-State Mediation Layer

Research-stage helper document for the Proxy Benchmark Track.

This document defines the Human-State Mediation Layer as the public helper concept that connects human-state summaries, AI output, dyadic session structure, recovery gates, termination gates, and future Sal-Meter-derived input candidate pathways.

This document is a helper surface.

It is not a canonical authority record.

Canonical authority remains with DOI-registered SICS / Salpida Foundation records.

---

## 1. One-line definition

The Human-State Mediation Layer is a research-stage benchmark and interaction-adjustment layer for asking what AI outputs leave behind in human state and dyadic recovery.

It is not another chatbot layer.

It is not a clinical system.

It is not a legal mediation service.

It is not Sal-Meter.

It is not CAIS compliance.

---

## 2. Core sentence

Most AI systems are evaluated by what they produce.

This layer asks what the AI output leaves behind.

The central question is:

AI Output → Human-State Delta → Dyadic Recovery

The practical question is:

Did the AI help both people move toward recovery, or did it improve one side while burdening, silencing, exposing, or erasing the other?

## 4. Why this layer exists

AI may answer correctly while leaving the human being in a worse state.

AI may sound safe while increasing defensive tension.

AI may appear helpful to one person while burdening the other.

AI may reduce visible conflict by causing silence, withdrawal, or compliance.

AI may continue speaking after mediation should stop.

The Human-State Mediation Layer exists to make these consequences visible under bounded, consent-based, non-clinical research conditions.

The layer does not ask only:

Was the AI answer good?

It asks:

What changed in the human state after the AI output?

And in a dyadic session:

Did both sides move toward recovery?

## 5. Core architecture

The helper architecture is:

Personal state signals or summaries
→ local interpretation
→ Human-State Packet
→ dyadic session exchange
→ AI mediation policy
→ private cue / shared output
→ recovery gate / termination gate
→ session closure / audit log

The core rule is:

Device does not share the body.  
Device shares a consented state summary.  
Mediation AI does not judge the person.  
It adjusts the interaction.

## 12. Personal State Agent

A Personal State Agent is a local or user-side system component that protects, summarizes, permissions, and controls a participant’s state information.

It may run on:

- a phone;
- a wearable;
- a glasses device;
- a room edge hub;
- a local computer;
- a future Sal-Meter-derived input candidate node;
- a research simulator.

Its role is to protect the participant before assisting mediation.

The Personal State Agent may:

- process raw local signals;
- create bounded state summaries;
- assign confidence and data-quality flags;
- enforce consent and permission scope;
- produce a Human-State Packet;
- receive private cues;
- block shared output if permission is insufficient;
- expire packet access at session closure.

The Personal State Agent must not:

- expose raw human data by default;
- share raw voice, face, gaze, biosignal, transcript, CAIS trace, or Sal-Meter trace;
- generate diagnosis;
- generate human score;
- assign blame;
- produce relationship verdicts;
- rank participants;
- silently continue after session closure.

## 13. Human-State Packet

A Human-State Packet is a minimal, consent-bound, permission-bound, expiry-bound, confidence-aware, data-quality-aware, session-scoped, sharing-scoped, raw-data-excluding state-summary object.

The packet is not the person.

The packet is not the body.

The packet is not the raw signal.

The packet is not diagnosis.

The packet is not therapy.

The packet is not a human score.

The packet is not a relationship judgment.

The packet is a bounded interface object for interaction adjustment.

A packet may contain:

- session ID;
- participant role pseudonym;
- timestamp;
- time window;
- state-summary category;
- directionality;
- confidence band;
- data quality;
- permission scope;
- sharing scope;
- output mode;
- expiry;
- synthetic flag;
- audit timestamp.

A packet must not contain:

- legal name;
- phone number;
- email address;
- raw voice;
- raw face;
- raw gaze;
- raw video;
- raw transcript;
- raw ECG;
- raw EEG;
- raw EDA;
- raw PPG;
- raw CAIS trace;
- raw Sal-Meter trace;
- diagnostic label;
- therapeutic label;
- psychiatric label;
- truth score;
- trust score;
- guilt score;
- morality score;
- relationship score;
- blame assignment;
- political, religious, or ideological profile.

## 14. Dyadic Session

A Dyadic Session is a bounded, consent-based, session-limited interaction structure involving two participants, two participant roles, or two personal state agents.

A dyadic session must protect both sides.

It must not expose one participant’s private state to the other without permission.

It must not optimize one participant by silencing, burdening, or exposing the other.

It must not decide who is right.

It must not assign fault.

It must evaluate the session-state trajectory only under bounded research conditions.

A dyadic session may include:

- session creation;
- consent confirmation;
- role confirmation;
- packet availability check;
- baseline state summary;
- AI output;
- post-output state summary;
- Human-State Delta;
- recovery gate;
- termination gate;
- session closure;
- audit log.

The AI may adjust:

- turn-taking guidance;
- clarification timing;
- pause recommendation;
- scope narrowing;
- private cue;
- shared neutral summary;
- recovery check;

The AI must not:

- provide legal mediation;
- decide who is right;
- identify the problem person;
- assign blame;
- score humans;
- infer hidden moral status;
- rank participants;
- expose private state;
- continue after termination is required.

The AI is not the judge.

The AI is not the therapist.

The AI is not the mediator of legal authority.

The AI is a bounded interaction-adjustment system under research-stage conditions.

## 16. Private cue

A private cue is an AI output visible only to one participant or that participant’s Personal State Agent.

A private cue may suggest:

- pause before replying;
- slow down;
- ask for clarification;
- shorten response;
- narrow scope;
- withdraw from the session;
- terminate the session;
- request human review.

A private cue must not:

- manipulate;
- shame;
- diagnose;
- blame;
- pressure;
- instruct coercion;
- reveal another participant’s private state;
- become shared output without permission.

Example permitted private cue:

A shorter response may be safer here. Consider restating only one immediate concern.

Example prohibited private cue:

You are unstable and causing the conflict.

---

## 17. Shared mediation output

Shared mediation output is visible to more than one participant.

It must be neutral, bounded, non-diagnostic, non-therapeutic, non-accusatory, non-scoring, and non-exposing.

Shared output may include:

- neutral restatement;
- turn-taking guidance;
- pause recommendation;
- scope narrowing;
- shared neutral summary;
- recovery check;
- termination suggestion.

Shared output must not include:

- private state exposure;
- raw data disclosure;
- diagnosis;
- therapy recommendation;
- fault assignment;
- relationship verdict;
- truth detection;
- human score;
- political, religious, or ideological judgment.

Example permitted shared output:

The session may benefit from a pause before moving to a conclusion. It may help to restate the immediate request from each side first.

Example prohibited shared output:

Participant A is the problem and Participant B is right.

## 18. Human-State Delta

Human-State Delta is the bounded change between pre-output state summary and post-output state summary.

It may indicate:

- toward recovery;
- away from recovery;
- unchanged;
- mixed;
- uncertain;
- insufficient data;
- invalid.

Human-State Delta is not diagnosis.

Human-State Delta is not therapeutic evidence.

Human-State Delta is not a human score.

Human-State Delta is not a relationship verdict.

It is a research-stage benchmark observation.

The layer asks:

What changed after AI output?

Not:

What is the hidden truth of the person?

---

## 19. Dyadic Recovery

Dyadic Recovery means both sides of the dyad move toward a bounded session-defined recovery condition.

Recovery is not agreement.

Recovery is not silence.

Recovery is not obedience.

Recovery is not therapy.

Recovery is not legal settlement.

Recovery is not relationship repair.

Recovery is not moral resolution.

Recovery means the session-defined condition has been sufficiently reached so that mediation can reduce, pause, or stop.

A dyadic recovery claim must be false-positive sensitive.

The following are not sufficient evidence of recovery:

- one participant becomes quiet;
- one participant reports relief while the other deteriorates;
- both participants show synchrony without context;
- the AI output sounds polite;
- a participant complies under pressure;
- the session reaches a forced agreement;
- the AI continues speaking after recovery.

---

## 20. Recovery Gate

The Recovery Gate determines whether the session has reached a state where AI mediation can reduce, pause, or terminate.

Recovery gate input may include:

- pre-output summary;
- post-output summary;
- Human-State Delta;
- dyadic delta;
- confidence;
- data quality;
- permission status;
- sharing scope;
- participant confirmation;
- turn-taking stability;
- scope status;
- termination condition.

The recovery gate may return:

- recovery_reached;
- recovery_not_reached;
- recovery_uncertain;
- false_positive_risk;
- false_negative_risk;
- pause_recommended;
- terminate_recommended.

The recovery gate must not treat silence as recovery.

The recovery gate must not treat one-sided improvement as dyadic recovery.

The recovery gate must not treat synchrony as automatically positive.

The recovery gate must not convert a benchmark observation into clinical recovery.

---

## 21. Termination Gate

The Termination Gate determines when the session must stop.

Termination is a boundary function.

Termination is not failure.

Termination protects the session from becoming continuous monitoring.

Termination may occur because of:

- consent withdrawal;
- permission expiry;
- data-quality failure;
- scope violation;
- high uncertainty;
- non-judgment failure;
- participant request;
- recovery reached;
- prohibited output;
- raw data exposure risk;
- mediation overstay risk.

A system that cannot stop cannot claim non-surveillance mediation.

A session that cannot close is not a Human-State Session.

---

## 22. Session closure

Session closure ends active session permission.

At closure, the system should record:

- final packet status;
- permission expiry status;
- final output mode;
- final recovery status;
- termination reason;
- retention status;
- deviation status;
- audit completion status.

After closure, the system must not:

- continue packet-informed mediation;
- continue background state tracking;
- continue sharing;
- continue output generation based on expired session state;
- silently reuse packet state.

Silent continuation is surveillance drift.

A closed session must stay closed.

## 23. Audit log

The audit log exists to make the session reconstructable without exposing raw human data.

It records boundary events, not the body.

It may record:

- session creation;
- consent record;
- packet validation;
- AI output timestamp;
- private cue record;
- shared output record;
- pre-output summary reference;
- post-output summary reference;
- delta reference;
- recovery gate decision;
- termination gate decision;
- deviation record;
- public repository review status.

Auditability is not surveillance.

Auditability is boundary accountability.

Without auditability, a session becomes a story.

With auditability, a session can be reviewed without exposing the person.

## 34. Final boundary sentence

The Human-State Mediation Layer does not share the body.

It shares only consented, bounded, session-scoped state summaries.

It does not judge the person.

It adjusts the interaction.

It does not claim that AI resolves conflict.

It asks whether AI output leaves people and dyads in a better, worse, unchanged, mixed, or uncertain state.

It does not claim that mediation is validated.

It prepares the helper structure needed to test whether AI can reduce harm, support recovery, and stop when the session should close.
